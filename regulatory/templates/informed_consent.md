## What is this study about?

We are asking you to participate in a research study that uses artificial intelligence (AI) to improve cancer treatment predictions. Your medical information (such as treatment details and test results) will be used to help train a computer program that may improve treatment decisions for future patients.

## How will my data be used?

- Your medical data will stay at this hospital — it will **never** be sent to another institution.
- Instead, we use a technique called "federated learning" where the computer program learns from data at multiple hospitals without the data ever leaving each hospital.
- Only mathematical summaries (called "model updates") are shared between hospitals, not your personal information.

## How is my privacy protected?

- All data used in this study will be "de-identified," meaning your name, date of birth, and other personal identifiers will be removed before any analysis.
- We use advanced privacy techniques (called "differential privacy") that add mathematical noise to prevent anyone from identifying individual patients from the model updates.
- An independent privacy officer monitors all data access.

## What are the risks?

- **This study poses minimal risk.** We are only analyzing existing medical records — there are no additional medical procedures or treatments.
- There is a very small theoretical risk that someone could try to extract personal information from the AI model, but our privacy protections are designed to prevent this.

## What are the benefits?

- You may not directly benefit from this study.
- The AI models developed may help future patients receive better treatment recommendations.

## Do I have to participate?

- **Participation is completely voluntary.**
- You can withdraw at any time without affecting your medical care.
- If you withdraw, your data will no longer be used in future model training (though it cannot be removed from models already trained).
